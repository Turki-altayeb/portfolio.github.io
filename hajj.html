Key Insights
  • 01 A direct correlation exists between pilgrim numbers and flight frequency, with a near 1:1 scaling pattern during peak Hajj season.
  • 02 Clear seasonal peaks emerge in the weeks preceding Hajj, with flight demand spiking up to 3x normal volume.
  • 03 Identified scheduling gaps where available flight capacity does not fully meet pilgrim demand, presenting optimization opportunities for airlines.
  • 04 Post-Hajj return flights show a congestion window of 5–7 days, suggesting staggered departure strategies could ease pressure.
